Course outline

Reading


Module 1: Premixed flames, with bunsen burner as an example: Tutorial

Notion of flame speed, global energy balance to derive relations between flame speed, thermochemistry and kinetics CKL $7.6.1, CKL $7.6.2, FAW $5.1.2,
Measurement of adiabatic flame speeds
Dependence of SL on various parameters CKL $1.4.6, CKL $7.7
1D conservation laws for steady laminar premixed flames General derivation: CKL $5.1/TP $2.3,
Low Mach number assumption: CKL $5.2.4.3
Flame speed calculations using FlameMaster PREMIX documentation
Flammability, Quenching, and ignition energy HSM 7.1, 7.2, 8.0
Turbulent premixed flames CKL $11.1, $11.2, $11.3, TP $5.3

Module 2: Non-premixed flames, with counterflow diffusion flame as an example

Using opp-diff burner as example, introduce counterflow non-premixed flames.
Governing equations in physical space, methodology for calculating opp-diff flame structure at different strain rates using FlameMaster.
Transformation of 1D conservation laws into mixture fraction space
Flamelet equation and the concept of scalar dissipation rate and its relation to reaction rates at different strain rates.
Turbulent non-premixed flames

Module 3: Burners for gaseous fuels

Domestic LPG stove burner - design concepts
MILD combustion - Theory of extinction of well-stirred reactors
Stationary gas turbines - Blow off criteria, design criteria

Module 4: Spray combustion and applications

Basics of atomization and types of injectors
Droplet combustion theory
Spray combustion: (i) Modifications to flamelet model to include droplets
Spray combustion: (ii) Aerodynamics and stability of spray flames
Spray combustion: (iii) Application in gas turbines and LP rockets

Module 5: Solid fuel combustion

Single particle combustion theory for coal and biomass
Combustion in pulverized coal burners
Combustion and gasification of coal in fluidized bed systems - design considerations
Flame propagation in packed beds and its application in design of moving grate furnaces
